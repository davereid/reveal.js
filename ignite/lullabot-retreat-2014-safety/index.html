Dehydration and Terminal Dehydration

Pool

From 2005-2009, there were an average of 3,533 fatal unintentional drownings (non-boating related) annually in the United States — about ten deaths per day. Nearly 80% of people who die from drowning are male. Children ages 1 to 4 have the highest drowning rates. Crunch the numbers for a swimming pool and you get about a one in 100,000 chance of dying in a swimming pool. If we were to continue our growth at our current rate, if we had a retreat at a pool in X years, someone will die.
